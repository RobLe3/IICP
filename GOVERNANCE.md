# IICP governance

IICP is stewarded as an open protocol rather than a proprietary platform.

## Authority

- The IICP specification repository is authoritative for protocol semantics,
  intent registries, profiles and compatibility rules.
- Component maintainers own implementation quality in their repositories.
- Production operators own their deployments and credentials.
- Organization ownership is administrative authority; it does not by itself
  grant authority to redefine the protocol.

Until a multi-person governance body is formally appointed, the original
maintainer acts as protocol chair and final release steward. Contributors may
review, implement, research and propose changes without receiving access to
private production systems.

## Change process

1. Open a public issue describing the interoperability problem.
2. Add evidence, alternatives and compatibility consequences.
3. Use an extension, profile or sub-protocol before changing the base wire
   contract unless evidence shows those mechanisms are insufficient.
4. Add conformance vectors and independent implementation evidence.
5. Record steward acceptance before declaring the change normative.

Repository administration, project-board participation and protocol decision
authority remain distinct roles.
